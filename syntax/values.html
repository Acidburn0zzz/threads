Values¶

Bytes¶

The simplest form of value are raw uninterpreted bytes. In the abstract syntax they are represented as hexadecimal literals.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{byte} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-byte}{\mathit{byte}} &::=& \def\mathdef1985#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1985{00} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1986#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1986{FF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Conventions¶

  • The meta variable \(b\) range over bytes.
  • Bytes are sometimes interpreted as natural numbers \(n < 256\).

Integers¶

Different classes of integers with different value ranges are distinguished by their size and their signedness.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{unsigned integer} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1932#1{{\mathit{u}#1}}\mathdef1932{N}} &::=& 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^N{-}1 \\ \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{signed integer} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1939#1{{\mathit{s}#1}}\mathdef1939{N}} &::=& -2^{N-1} ~|~ \dots ~|~ {-}1 ~|~ 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^{N-1}{-}1 \\ \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{uninterpreted integer} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1944#1{{\mathit{i}#1}}\mathdef1944{N}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1932#1{{\mathit{u}#1}}\mathdef1932{N}} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The latter class defines uninterpreted integers, whose signedness interpretation can vary depending on context. In the abstract syntax, they are represented as unsigned. However, some operations convert them to signed based on a two’s complement interpretation.

Conventions¶

  • The meta variables \(m, n, i\) range over integers.
  • Numbers may be denoted by simple arithmetics, as in the grammar above.

Floating-Point¶

Floating-point data consists of 32 or 64 bit values according to the IEEE 754 standard. Every value has a sign and a magnitude.

Magnitudes can either be expressed as normal numbers of the form \(m_0.m_1m_2\dots m_M \cdot2^e\), where \(m\) is the significand whose most signifcant bit \(m_0\) is \(1\), and \(e\) is the exponent, or as a subnormal number where \(m_0\) is \(0\) and the exponent is fixed to the smallest possible value; among the subnormals are positive and negative zero. Since the significands are binary values, normals are represented in the form \((1 + m\cdot 2^{-M})\), where \(M\) is the bit width of \(m\); similarly for subnormals.

Possible magnitudes also include the special values \(\infty\) (infinity) and \(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\mathsf{nan}}\) (NaN, not a number). NaN values have a payload value that describes the mantissa bits in the underlying binary representation. No distinction is made between signalling and quiet NaNs.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llcll} \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{floating-point number} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\def\mathdef1947#1{{\mathit{f}#1}}\mathdef1947{N}} &::=& {+} \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\def\mathdef1948#1{{\mathit{f}#1}}\mathdef1948{\mathit{Nmag}}} ~|~ {-} \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\def\mathdef1948#1{{\mathit{f}#1}}\mathdef1948{\mathit{Nmag}}} \\ \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{floating-point magnitude} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\def\mathdef1948#1{{\mathit{f}#1}}\mathdef1948{\mathit{Nmag}}} &::=& (1 + \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1933#1{{\mathit{u}#1}}\mathdef1933{M}}\cdot 2^{-M}) \cdot 2^e & (M = \href{../syntax/values.html#aux-significand}{\mathrm{signif}}(N) \wedge -2^{\href{../syntax/values.html#aux-exponent}{\mathrm{expon}}(N)-1}+2 \leq e \leq 2^{\href{../syntax/values.html#aux-exponent}{\mathrm{expon}}(N)-1}-1) \\ &&|& (0 + \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1933#1{{\mathit{u}#1}}\mathdef1933{M}}\cdot 2^{-M}) \cdot 2^e & (M = \href{../syntax/values.html#aux-significand}{\mathrm{signif}}(N) \wedge e = -2^{\href{../syntax/values.html#aux-exponent}{\mathrm{expon}}(N)-1}+2) \\ &&|& \infty \\ &&|& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\mathsf{nan}}(n) & (1 \leq n < 2^{\href{../syntax/values.html#aux-significand}{\mathrm{signif}}(N)}) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

where

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lclllllcl} \href{../syntax/values.html#aux-significand}{\mathrm{signif}}(32) &=& 23 &&&& \href{../syntax/values.html#aux-exponent}{\mathrm{expon}}(32) &=& 8 \\ \href{../syntax/values.html#aux-significand}{\mathrm{signif}}(64) &=& 52 &&&& \href{../syntax/values.html#aux-exponent}{\mathrm{expon}}(64) &=& 11 \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

A canonical NaN is a floating-point value \(\pm\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\mathsf{nan}}(\href{../syntax/values.html#aux-canon}{\mathrm{canon}}_N)\) where \(\href{../syntax/values.html#aux-canon}{\mathrm{canon}}_N\) is a payload whose most significant bit is \(1\) while all others are \(0\):

\[\href{../syntax/values.html#aux-canon}{\mathrm{canon}}_N = 2^{\href{../syntax/values.html#aux-significand}{\mathrm{signif}}_N-1}\]

An arithmetic NaN is a floating-point value \(\pm\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\mathsf{nan}}(n)\) with \(n > \href{../syntax/values.html#aux-canon}{\mathrm{canon}}_N\), such that the most significant bit is \(1\) while all others are arbitrary.

Conventions¶

  • The meta variable \(z\) ranges over floating-point values where clear from context.
  • Floating-point numbers, in normal or subnormal form, are sometimes interpreted as rational numbers \(q \in \mathbb{Q}\).

Vectors¶

Vectors are bounded sequences of the form \(A^n\) (or \(A^\ast\)), where the \(A\)-s can either be values or complex constructions. A vector can have at most \(2^{32}-1\) elements.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lllll} \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{vector} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-vec}{\mathit{vec}}(A) &::=& A^n & (n < 2^{32})\\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Names¶

Names are sequences of scalar Unicode code points.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll} \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{name} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{name}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{codepoint}}^\ast & (|\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{codepoint}}^\ast)| < 2^{32}) \\ \def\mathdef1931#1{{}}\mathdef1931{code point} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{codepoint}} &::=& \def\mathdef1987#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1987{0000} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1988#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1988{D7FF} ~|~ \def\mathdef1989#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1989{E000} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1990#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1990{10FFFF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Due to the limitations of the binary format, the lengths of a name is bounded by the length of its UTF-8 encoding. The auxiliary \(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}\) function expressing this encoding is defined as follows:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lcl@{\qquad}l} \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c^\ast) &=& (\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c))^\ast \\[1ex] \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c) &=& b & (c < \def\mathdef1991#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1991{80} \wedge c = b) \\ \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c) &=& b_1~b_2 & (\def\mathdef1992#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1992{80} \leq c < \def\mathdef1993#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1993{800} \wedge c = 2^6\cdot(b_1-\def\mathdef1994#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1994{C0})+(b_2-\def\mathdef1995#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1995{80})) \\ \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c) &=& b_1~b_2~b_3 & (\def\mathdef1996#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1996{800} \leq c < \def\mathdef1997#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1997{10000} \wedge c = 2^{12}\cdot(b_1-\def\mathdef1998#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1998{C0})+2^6\cdot(b_2-\def\mathdef1999#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1999{80})+(b_3-\def\mathdef2000#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef2000{80})) \\ \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c) &=& b_1~b_2~b_3~b_4 & (\def\mathdef2001#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2001{10000} \leq c < \def\mathdef2002#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2002{110000} \wedge c = 2^{18}\cdot(b_1-\def\mathdef2003#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef2003{C0})+2^{12}\cdot(b_2-\def\mathdef2004#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef2004{80})+2^6\cdot(b_3-\def\mathdef2005#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef2005{80})+(b_4-\def\mathdef2006#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef2006{80})) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Convention¶

  • Code points are sometimes used interchangeably with natural numbers \(n < 1114112\).
